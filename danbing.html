The History of Dan Bing & Granjon

Dan Bing is attributed to being a Taiwanese development of the traditional Jian Bing, a Chinese egg crepe thought to have originated in the Shandong Province from military commander Zhuge Liang after watching his soldiers use their shields to cook after losing their woks.

Granjon, an Old Style typeface, is the perfect complement to the traditional Taiwanese dish. Designed in 1928 by George W. Jones, this serif typeface is known for its delicate, incisor-like serifs, high stroke contrast, ascender capped with downward slope, and vertical stress.

Recipe

Ingredients

three eggs
one half cup bread flour
two tbsp. corn starch
one cup water
three fourths tsp. sesame oil
three tbsp. scallions
some soy paste or chili sauce

Steps

Mix together your dry ingredients in a small bowl. Add water, mix well, and let batter rest for 10 minutes.

For each crepe, whisk 1 egg with 1/4 tsp. sesame oil, 1 tbsp. of water, and a tbsp. of chopped scallions.

Heat a non-stick pan to medium with oil. Stir the batter up and add a third of it into the pan.

Coat the pan with a thin layer of batter, and cook the crepe until it’s set and the edges begin to peel back.

Flip the crepe onto a plate, and then flip it back into the pan to cook the other side.

Pour the egg mixture on top of the crepe. be careful to not let egg go over the edge.

Cook until the egg is set and flip the crepe with a spatula. Cook for 10-20 more seconds.

When done, flip the crepe egg side up on a plate or cutting board.

Cut into sections and drizzle on your sauce of choice.
