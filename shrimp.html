The History of Shrimp & Grits & Abril

Eating Shrimp and Grits elicits a rich taste and Southern traditions. Grits’ origins are traced to the Native American Muskogee tribe’s preparation of Indian corn, grinding the corn in a stone mill, giving it its familiar “gritty” texture. There are even known writings from West African slave descendants that mention meals resembling shrimp and grits. Still, it wasn’t until Crook’s Corner chef Bill Neal’s recipe was published in The New York Times that the humble dish gained popularity nationwide.

Abril Text was conceived by designers Veronika Burian and José Scaglione taking inspiration from 19th century slab serifs and Scotch Roman typefaces. It evokes an older, elegant feel, but still features a more contemporary look and feel. It’s a little formal, but has enough swing in its serifs to make it a Southern Belle.

Recipe

Ingredients

12 yellow shrimp
one pt. yellow grits
one cup sharp white cheddar, grated
four oz. unsalted butter
one qt. chicken stock
eight oz. smoked andouille sausage, small dice
two roma tomatoes, concasse
six oz. burning river pale ale
one shallot, minced
one bu. scallions, sliced thin
one fresh lime

Steps

Simmer chicken stock and slowly whisk in grits. Stir every few minutes to prevent sticking.

Saute andouille in vegetable oil until brown and crispy. Remove and set aside.

Season shrimp with salt and pepper. Sear in residual andouille fat and remove just before the center becomes opaque.

Drain excess fat and sweat shallots until aroma develops.

Deglaze with beer and add 1/2 C chicken stock. Simmer until reduced by ¾.

Add tomatoes, shrimp and andouille to sauce. Season to taste and finish with fresh lime juice.

Whisk butter and cheese into grits.

Spoon grits onto the center of the plate and top with 6 shrimp. Pour sauce over top and garnish with scallions.
