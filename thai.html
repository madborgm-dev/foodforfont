The History of Thai Green Curry & Futura

Thai Green Curry is one of the many favorite curry dishes in Thai restaurants. Most curries are based on pastes made from grinding up fresh and dried herbs and spices. Considered to be native to India, the methods for cooking curry were brought to South East Asia by Indian immigrants over the past several centuries. Curry is a staple dish in many homes for its use of inexpensive and healthy ingredients.

Futura was one of the many typeface designs influenced by the minimalist aesthetic of the Bauhaus movement. German type designer Paul Renner designed the font between 1924 and 1926 and released the font in 1927. Futura makes use of basic geometric proportions with no serifs or frills.

Recipe

Ingredients

three fourths cup sliced shallots
one and three fourths tsp. Thai Green Curry Paste
one can unsweetened coconut milk
two tsp. fish sauce
one lb. chicken thighs
one large red bell pepper
one fourth cup chopped basil sprigs
two tbsp. fresh lime juice

Steps

In a large saucepan, heat one tbsp. of vegetable oil over medium heat.

Once the vegetable oil is hot, add three fourths cup sliced shallots and one and three fourths tsp. of green curry paste.

Stir the mixture until the shallots soften, about two minutes.

Add one 14-ounce can of unsweetened coconut milk and fish sauce to the pan with the shallots.

Bring the curry paste, shallots, coconut milk, and fish sauce in the pan to a boil.

Add chicken and bell pepper to the pan, stir until the chicken is just cooked through.

Once the chicken is just cooked through, stir in one fourth chopped fresh basil and two tbsps. fresh lime juice.

Divide curry among bowls of cooked white rice. Garnish with basil and lime juice.
