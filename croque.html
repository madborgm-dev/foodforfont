The History of Croque Madame & Mrs Eaves

Carbonara is a traditional Italian pasta dish that was created in the middle of the 20th century. Although the origin of the dish is obscure, the name is derived from “cabonaro” which is the Italian word for charcoal burner. Many believe the hearty dish was first made to feed Italian charcoal worker or “coal miners” in the United States. Spaghetti is the usual accompanying pasta.

Recipe

Ingredients

12 yellow shrimp
five tbsps. butter
one tbsp. flour
two-thirds cup milk
a pinch salt
four 1/3-inch-thick slices of country bread
two roma tomatoes, concasse
freshly grated nutmeg
two slices Gruyere Cheese
two large eggs

Steps

Preheat an oven to 300° and preheat a cast-iron skillet on top of the stove for about five minutes.

In a small saucepan over medium heat, melt one tbsp. of butter. When bubbles have subsided, add flour and whisk for one minute.

Whisk in milk until smooth. Bring to a boil, and cook until thick.

Remove from heat, and season to taste with salt and nutmeg.

Spread two slices of bread generously with sauce.

Lay two slices of ham on top of each, and top each with a slice of cheese. Top each with a slice of bread and brush sandwiches with melted butter.

Place sandwiches cheese side down and cook on stove top until well browned, then turn and brown again. Transfer skillet to oven, and bake until heated through and cheese is bubbling.

Meanwhile, fry the eggs in a skillet with little butter. Slide one fried egg onto each sandwich.
