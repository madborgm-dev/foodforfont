The History of Double Pork Carnitas & Mission District

Carnitas translates directly as “little meats” in Spanish. The traditional Mexican cuisine dish originates from the state of Michoacán. What makes Carnitas special is the way they are cooked, typically made by braising or simmering pork in oil or lard for three to four hours until the meat is very tender and juicy. Michoacan carnitas require “Hierbas de Olor,” (Herbs that smell) such as bay leaf, thyme and marjoram for the traditional flavors.

The idea and influences for Mission Gothic came during a trip designers James T. Edmonson and Trevor Baum took with the Lost Type Co. to San Francisco’s Mission District. The area is known as “The Mission,” was colonized in the late 18th century by Spanish missionaries. The area’s Spanish heritage is seen throughout the neighborhood and localized styling of Mexican food.

Recipe

Ingredients

two lbs. pork shoulder
two tsps. salt
two tsps. ground black pepper
two tsps. dried oregano (preferably mexican)
one half large onion
one avocado, halved, pitted, sliced
fresh cilantro sprigs
one sliced red bell pepper
one packet corn tortillas

Steps

Toss pork in bowl of slow cooker with slat, black pepper, and dried oregano to coat.

Cut the onion into four pieces. Place the onion atop the pork in the slow cooker.

Cover the slow cooker and cook the pork on low setting until the meat is as tender as possible. It should take 6 hours for the meat to be falling apart.

Using a slotted spoon, transfer the tender pork to a cutting board. Discard the onion.

Using your fingers, shred the pork. Transfer carnitas to a platter.

Place the avocado slices, cilantro sprigs, and sliced red bell peppers, if desired, alongside.

Wrap the corn tortillas in a damp kitchen towel. Microwave them for about one minute or until they are warm to the touch.

Serve the carnitas with the warm tortillas and your choice of salsa and/or toppings.
