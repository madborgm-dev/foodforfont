The History of Spaghetti Carbonara & DDC Hardware

Carbonara is a traditional Italian pasta dish that was created in the middle of the 20th century. Although the origin of the dish is obscure, the name is derived from “cabonaro” which is the Italian word for charcoal burner. Many believe the hearty dish was first made to feed Italian charcoal worker or “coal miners” in the United States. Spaghetti is the usual accompanying pasta.

DDC Hardware is a display typeface designed and released by Aaron James Draplin and Riley Cran in 2017 through the Lost Type Co. After searching for a font that was “painfully functional and unapologetic in its lack of “the latest stuff,” Draplin drew inspiration for DDC Hardware from old steel siding or run-down factory brick walls. The utilitarian typeface represents a tough, no nonsense time. THE typeface comes in three separate weights, regular, condensed and compressed.

Recipe

Ingredients

12 yellow shrimp
one pt. yellow grits
one cup sharp white cheddar, grated
four oz. unsalted butter
one qt. chicken stock
eight oz. smoked andouille sausage, small dice
two roma tomatoes, concasse
six oz. burning river pale ale
one shallot, minced
one bu. scallions, sliced thin
one fresh lime

Steps

Simmer chicken stock and slowly whisk in grits. Stir every few minutes to prevent sticking.

Saute andouille in vegetable oil until brown and crispy. Remove and set aside.

Season shrimp with salt and pepper. Sear in residual andouille fat and remove just before the center becomes opaque.

Drain excess fat and sweat shallots until aroma develops.

Deglaze with beer and add 1/2 C chicken stock. Simmer until reduced by 3/4.

Add tomatoes, shrimp and andouille to sauce. Season to taste and finish with fresh lime juice.

Whisk butter and cheese into grits.

Spoon grits onto the center of the plate and top with 6 shrimp. Pour sauce over top and garnish with scallions.
